Brandon, as you know, people affected by MS — including caregivers and loved ones — face a heavy financial toll every day. In fact, a new study commissioned by the National MS Society confirmed that the average total cost of living with multiple sclerosis is a staggering $88,487 per year.
They wanted $900 a month for the brand name (DMT) and $700 a month for the generic. That is not an amount I can pay. I was off it for 3 months, and because of that, I have gone from using a cane full time to now having to use a walker.”

- Holly, diagnosed in 2011
I received this tiny little (DMT) pill that cost $7,000, and I remember thinking, ‘This is like a diamond,’ in terms of value and size, and I thought, ‘How do people afford this?’”

- Eric, diagnosed in 2013
When people think of caregiving, images of helping with daily life may come to mind. But something people may not think about as much: his main responsibility in my MS care is to provide financial support.”
- Monica, diagnosed in 2007
For Holly, Monica, Eric and so many others who are feeling the financial burden of MS, I hope you will become an MS Activist. Their stories matter and so does yours. As one of our Activists, you can let lawmakers know how the cost of MS affects you — your story can change lives!

Thank you for all you do,
